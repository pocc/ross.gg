# Hamilton
Cool city. Capital of Bermuda, and it became the capital from St Georges in <year>. The ferry uses this city as a hub to get to other parts of the island. 

# St Georges
*St Georges rhymes with "Gorgeous"*. The cannons that flank a statue of St Georges point towards the city, and this is not due to needing to protect aristocracy, but purely for decoration. St Georges looks like a wild man, but one would presume that most people who lived 400 years ago were a little more wild.


# Gibbs Lighthouse
This was in use prior to the advent of GPS navigation. 

185 steps, which just keeps on going. Feels like a castle turret with the circular staircase. At the top though, you get a view of most of the entire island, albeit through the structural beams and wiring.

It has the cheapest gifts in Bermuda according to our tour guide, and I found this to be true compared to St Georges (2/3 as much for similar items).
